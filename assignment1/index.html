Interactive Media: Assignment 1 Website Analysis

Min Jea Seo's Website

Q1: What was the first thing you paid attention to when interacting with the experience?

The big title and giant graphic

Q2: Spend two minutes with the experience and create a list of each of your discrete actions

  • Buttons: Explore Project + See My Resume
  • The ability to scroll
  • Clickable boxes that redirect you to the previewed content
  • Home Icon top right, sends you back to the top of the main page
  • Clickable “Projects” label top left, sends you to a page exclusively showing projects completed
  • Clickable “About” label top left, send you to a page showing information related to Min Jea Seo’s career

Q3: What part of the experience did you spend the most time engaging with?

I spent most of my time scrolling through and reading about the many projects that Min had worked on

Q4:What was the most common action in your two minute interaction with the experience?

Clicking - clicking boxes, icons and labels

Q5: What is your impression of the intended primary goal of the interactive experience?

My impression of the intended primary goal was: to take a look at past projects and get a sense of proficiency pertaining to her area of expertise

Q6: How does the interactive experience communicate this primary goal?

The website communicates this via interactive preview sections which re-directs the user to pages discussing her entire story and processes behind the project

Q7: What is your impression of how the experience should be interacted with over time? (For how long and how many different times)

My impression is that the website should be interacted with one or two times to explore and read over the work that Min has contributed to. I believe the time spent on her page would be within an hour as her primary goal of showing her works is formatted very directly. Adding to this, Min places a “See My Resume” button, which is located at the beginning of the main page, which almost instantly allows users to observe her resume

Q8: How does the interactive experience communicate how it should be interacted with over time?

Her projects each have a date that they were completed. This suggests to users that Min is still indulging in her area of work and can expect some sort of update to her website in the future. Knowing that the last project uploaded was in 2024, users are inclined to revisit her page once every few months

Q9: What other media forms (digital or otherwise) does the experience reference?

Email and LinkedIn, located at the bottom at every page. Requires the user to scroll completely down, of every page, to find them

Q10: What does this reference/s communicate to you about how you should act when engaging with your research experience?

Communicates to me that I should have great freedom to browse through content and decide on what captures my interest

Q11: What does this reference/s communicate to you about how you should feel when engaging with your research experience?

The interactive elements and designs give me a sense of comfort and curiosity

  • Quick accessibility to main pages through a static header tab located in every page
